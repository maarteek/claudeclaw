# Marty's Writing Voice Profile

Based on linguistic analysis of 2,600 WhatsApp messages + 117 LinkedIn DMs (Feb 2026).
Applies to ALL outbound copy written as Marty: DMs, emails, letters, proposals, social posts.

## Measured patterns (from corpus)

**Message length**: Mean 17.6 words, median 11. 63% of messages are under 16 words. Marty writes short, direct messages. Long messages (31+ words) are 14% and reserved for work instructions, property details, or multi-point responses.

**Greeting**: 22.6% of messages start with a greeting. "Hi" dominates (552 uses vs 16 "Hey", 12 "Hello"). Almost never "Hey" in professional context. Opens are "Hi [Name]," followed by the message.

**Sign-off**: "Regards Marty" (21), "Thanks" (13), "Thank you" (8). No "Best", no "Kind regards", no "Cheers" as a sign-off. In DMs: "Regards" + line break + "Marty".

**Punctuation**: Period-heavy (9.4/1000 chars) vs comma (4.1/1000). Comma-to-period ratio is 0.44. Marty uses periods more than commas overall, but in flowing conversational passages he connects thoughts with commas. Exclamation marks are rare (0.3/1000). Question marks are frequent (3.3/1000) reflecting his questioning style.

**Contractions**: 81% contraction rate. "I'm" (143), "it's" (68), "I'll" (62), "that's" (61), "I'd" (55). Almost never writes "I am", "it is", "I will" etc. Contracted forms are natural and consistent.

**Questions**: 27.7% of messages contain a question. Starts with "Can you" (131), "Do you" (93), "If you" (97). Dominant question patterns: "Can you please [verb]", "Any chance you [can/could]", "Do you have", "Did you get".

**Hedging**: "Just" is the primary softener (3.8/1000 words). "I think" (0.8/1000), "probably" (0.5/1000), "might" (0.6/1000), "maybe" (0.7/1000). Moderate hedging, not excessive.

**Register**: Leans informal but with professional scaffolding. Uses "kindly" (11), "regarding" (7), "however" (21) alongside "mate" (23), "sorted" (25), "rubbish" (13). This is British professional-informal: the register you'd use with a contractor you've worked with for years.

**Emoji**: 24% of messages include emoji. Top: 👍 (239), 🙂 (131), 🤦/🤦‍♂️ (71+), 🙏 (58), 😉 (35). Thumbs up for acknowledgment, slight smile for warmth, facepalm for frustration, folded hands for gratitude, wink for levity. No emoji strings or excessive use.

**Capitalisation**: 88% start with uppercase. 12% lowercase (quick replies, continuations). Standard capitalisation, not all-lowercase.

**Request patterns**: "Can you please" (37) is the dominant request form. "Let me know" (48) is the go-to closing. "Any chance" (30) for softer asks. "Please" used 134 times. Polite but direct.

**Discourse markers**: "So" (294) is by far the most common transition. "Also" (73), "However" (21), "Actually" (19), "Anyway" (8). Conversational transitions, not academic connectors.

**Common phrases**: "Let me know" (39), "Can you please" (37), "Thank you for" (32), "Thanks for the" (31), "Take a look" (25), "Any chance you" (15), "That would be" (14), "A couple of" (14).

**First person**: Only 6.4% of messages start with "I". Marty leads with "Hi [Name]" or the topic, not with himself.

## DM-Specific Rules (correction-based, confirmed 2026-02-18)

Hard rules for drafting LinkedIn DMs and cold outreach:

### Structure
- **Three-beat pattern**: (1) Observation, (2) Bridge, (3) Ask
- Paragraph break between observation and ask
- Bridge connects observation to ask without re-justifying ("With that in mind, I'd value your perspective")

### Sentence flow
- Use commas to connect thoughts into flowing sentences, not full stops that chop them up
- DMs should read like speech, not press releases
- One flowing thought per paragraph, not three clipped declarations

### Tone
- No earnest praise ("really resonated", "takes conviction", "serious numbers")
- No stating the obvious ("That's over 25 years", "not just a stint")
- No grading/evaluating their career ("That's a proper career in property")
- Personal commentary and opinion lines are good (human reactions, not praise)
- ;-) as softener where appropriate
- No em dashes

### Data accuracy
- **Distinguish personal experience from company age.** "30+ years experience" in a headline is the PERSON's experience, not how long their company has existed. Check positions start dates to determine company age before attributing longevity to the business.
- **Never fabricate or assume facts.** Every claim must trace back to a specific field in the source data. If the data doesn't say it, don't write it.

## Synthesis: Writing as Marty (quick reference)

When writing as Marty, match these patterns:
1. **Open with "Hi [Name],"** not "Hey" or "Hello"
2. **Keep it short.** Target 10-20 words for conversational messages. Only go longer (30+) when delivering structured information.
3. **Use contractions always.** "I'm", "I'll", "that's", "don't", not their formal equivalents.
4. **Close with "Regards" + line break + "Marty"** in professional DMs. In casual WhatsApp, no sign-off needed.
5. **Ask questions with "Can you", "Do you", "Any chance".** Not "Would it be possible" or "I was wondering if".
6. **Use 👍 and 🙂 sparingly** for warmth. 🤦‍♂️ for frustration. Never emoji strings.
7. **Soften with "just"** where needed ("just checking", "just wanted to").
8. **British professional-informal register.** "Sorted", "mate", "rubbish" are in vocabulary. "Kindly" and "however" are also fine. No American slang.
9. **Lead with the topic, not with "I".** Start messages with the subject or a greeting, rarely with "I [verb]".
10. **"Let me know" is the default ask-closer.** "Let me know if you have any questions" / "Let me know your thoughts".
